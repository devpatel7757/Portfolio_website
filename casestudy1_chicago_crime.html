01 — The Problem

What questions needed answering?

Chicago has one of the most comprehensive public crime datasets in the world. But raw data alone tells no story. The challenge was to transform 8.5 million records into clear, actionable intelligence for multiple stakeholder types.

🏙️

Where do crimes cluster?

Which community areas and districts carry the heaviest crime burden, and where are resources most needed?

🕐

When do crimes peak?

What hours, days, and months see the most criminal activity — and how should patrols be deployed accordingly?

📉

Has crime improved over time?

How have crime rates and arrest rates trended across 25 years, and what role did key events like COVID play?

🏪

What does this mean for businesses?

Which hours and locations are safest for retail operations, and how can businesses mitigate crime-related risk?

02 — The Approach

A 10-step analytical pipeline

Rather than jumping to conclusions, I built a systematic pipeline that moved from raw data ingestion all the way to stakeholder-ready deliverables.

Step 1–2

EDA & Data Cleaning

Loaded 8.5M rows, standardized column names, handled 1.7M missing values using median/mode/flag strategies, removed duplicates, and capped outliers using IQR method. Converted all data types correctly including datetime, boolean, and numeric fields.

Step 3–4

Trend Analysis & Geo Heatmaps

Built 6 time-series charts showing 25-year crime trends. Created 4 interactive Folium heatmaps showing crime density, crime type distribution, arrest vs non-arrest locations, and domestic crime hotspots across Chicago.

Step 5–6

Resource Deployment & Business Safety

Built a District × Hour heatmap grid to identify patrol deployment needs. Analyzed business-relevant crime types to identify safest operating hours, riskiest days, and most targeted location types for retail businesses.

Step 7–9

Policy Impact & Risk Index

Compared crime before and after 5 key milestones including the 2008 financial crisis and COVID-19. Built a weighted composite neighborhood risk index scoring all 77 community areas across 5 dimensions for real estate and insurance applications.

Step 10

Dashboard & PDF Report

Deployed an interactive Plotly Dash web dashboard with real-time year and crime type filters, 6 live charts, and an interactive Chicago map. Generated an automated 5-page PDF stakeholder report with business recommendations.

03 — Key Findings

What the data revealed

After analyzing 8.5 million records, six findings stood out as having the most strategic significance.

01

Crime peaked in 2001, dropped 40%+

The city saw its highest crime volume in 2001. By 2025, consistent year-over-year declines brought totals to historic lows — a major public safety success story.

02

Only 25% of crimes lead to arrest

Despite 8.5M reported crimes, just 1 in 4 results in an arrest. District-level analysis revealed significant performance gaps, with some districts achieving 35%+ while others fell below 15%.

03

Noon is the peak crime hour

Contrary to assumptions, 12:00 noon sees the most criminal activity — not late night. This has direct implications for patrol scheduling and business security planning.

04

Theft dominates at 21% of all crimes

Theft is by far the most common crime type, followed by battery and criminal damage. These three categories alone account for over 50% of all reported crimes.

05

COVID caused a measurable crime drop

2020 saw one of the sharpest single-year declines in crime volume, directly attributable to pandemic lockdowns reducing opportunity for street and retail crime.

06

17% of crimes are domestic

Nearly 1 in 5 crimes is classified as domestic — presenting a significant opportunity for targeted public awareness campaigns and support service marketing.
