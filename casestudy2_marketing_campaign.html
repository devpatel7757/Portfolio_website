01 The Challenge

Marketing spend without measurement is guesswork

The business was running campaigns across email, social media, and paid ads — but had no unified view of performance. Budget allocation was based on intuition, not data. The challenge was to build a framework that answered three hard questions.

💸

Where is the budget being wasted?

Which channels and campaigns were consuming spend without delivering proportional returns in conversions or revenue?

👥

Which customer segments are most valuable?

Are all customers equally worth acquiring, or are some segments dramatically more engaged and higher-value than others?

📈

How can ROI be systematically improved?

What specific, data-backed interventions would reduce Customer Acquisition Cost while maintaining or growing conversion volume?

02 The Methodology

From raw campaign data to strategic insight

Step 01

Data Extraction & Cleaning

Used SQL and Python/Pandas to extract multichannel campaign data, clean inconsistencies, standardize date formats, and merge channel-level datasets into a single analytical view.

Step 02

KPI Framework Design

Defined and calculated four core metrics: Click-Through Rate (CTR), Conversion Rate, Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), and Return on Investment (ROI) — broken down by channel, campaign, and segment.

Step 03

Segment Analysis

Identified high-performing customer segments using engagement scoring. Compared behavior patterns across demographic and behavioral segments to isolate groups with 30%+ above-average engagement.

Step 04

Power BI Dashboard

Built an interactive Power BI dashboard visualizing all KPIs with drill-down capability by channel, time period, and customer segment — enabling non-technical stakeholders to self-serve insights.

04 Key Insights

What the analysis uncovered

Win

Email ROI is 5× paid ads

Email campaigns deliver 320% ROI vs just 62% for paid ads — suggesting a significant reallocation opportunity from paid to email.

Gap

Paid ads budget overspend

Paid advertising was consuming a disproportionate budget share despite producing the lowest conversion rate and highest CAC across all channels.

Opportunity

Top segments have 30%+ edge

High-value segments showed consistently higher engagement and lower CAC — targeting these segments specifically could reduce blended CAC by 15–20%.
