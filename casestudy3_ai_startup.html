01 Objective

Mapping the global AI startup landscape

The AI and data startup ecosystem is expanding rapidly but unevenly. Investors, founders, and analysts need to understand where capital is concentrating, which sectors are drawing the most funding, and what structural factors (like team size) predict startup success.

This project used AngelList data on 497 global startups to answer those questions systematically — using R for the complete data pipeline from cleaning to visualization.

04 Key Findings

What the data revealed about the ecosystem

📈

Team size strongly predicts funding

A strong positive correlation was found between team size and total funding raised — startups with 10+ employees received disproportionately larger investment rounds.

🌍

Berlin and Bangalore are emerging fast

While SF dominates overall, Berlin (Europe) and Bangalore (Asia) showed the fastest growth rates in new startup formation and funding inflows over the analysis period.

💰

FinTech commands premium valuations

AI-powered FinTech startups commanded the highest average funding rounds — approximately 2.4× the dataset average — driven by regulatory tailwinds and large addressable markets.

🔬

Healthcare AI is the fastest-growing sector

Though currently ranked 2nd in total funding, Healthcare AI showed the steepest growth curve — suggesting it may overtake FinTech as the dominant sector within 3–5 years.

05 Methodology

The R analytics pipeline

The entire analysis was built end-to-end in R — from raw data ingestion through cleaning, feature engineering, and publication-ready visualizations using ggplot2.

Key pipeline steps included: parsing funding values from mixed-format strings (e.g. "$2.5M", "$500K"), standardizing employee range fields into numeric midpoints, geocoding startup locations, and building multi-layer ggplot2 charts with custom themes for each visualization.
